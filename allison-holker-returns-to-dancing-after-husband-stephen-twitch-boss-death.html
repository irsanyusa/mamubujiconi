Allison Holker Returns to Dancing After Husband Stephen 'tWitch' Boss' Death

TALENTRECAP.COM Entertainment Celebrity

Photo by Rodin Eckenroth/Getty Images

Reading Time: 2 minutes

A year after the death of her husband, Stephen “tWitch” Boss, former Dancing With the Stars professional dancer Allison Holker has returned to dancing. In a recent Instagram post, Holker shared a video of herself dancing with her friend, choreographer Brittany Russell. The two performed a quick routine to the song “Pass That Dutch” by Missy Elliot.

Allison Holker Posts First Dance Routine Since Husband’s Death

Dance has always been a source of joy and expression for Holker, and it was no doubt a comfort to her during the difficult time following her husband’s death. Her return to dancing is also a sign of her strength and resilience. It shows that she is not letting her grief define her, and that she is determined to move forward with her life.

Holker’s return to dancing is a significant step in her healing process. She has said that dance was one of the things that helped her through her husband’s death, and it is clear that it is still an important part of her life.

In an interview following her husband’s death, Holker said that she knew she would eventually get back to dancing. “He’s guiding me on this path,” she said of her husband.

In addition to her return to dancing, Holker has also been outspoken about mental health awareness and suicide prevention. She has shared her own story of struggling with depression and anxiety, and she has encouraged others to seek help if they are struggling.

Allison Holker Previously Said She Hadn’t Danced Since tWitch’s Death

Holker and Boss were known for their love of dance. They would often share choreographed dance videos on social media, which earned them a significant fanbase.

After Boss’s death, Holker said that she wasn’t sure if she would ever dance again. However, in the months since his death, she has slowly started to come back to it.

“It’s been a process,” Holker said. “But I’m starting to feel more comfortable with it. I’m starting to feel like myself again.”

Holker’s return to dancing is a significant step in her healing process. It is a way for her to connect with her husband’s memory and to find joy in something that they both loved doing together.
